EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This analysis examines whether rainfall levels affect the fatality rate
of traffic accidents in Turkey between 2020 and 2024.

The hypothesis tested is:
Higher rainfall leads to higher fatality rates in traffic accidents.

HYPOTHESIS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
H1: Fatal traffic accident rates increase during months with higher rainfall.

METHOD:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Monthly traffic accident data (2020–2024) was combined with
  monthly precipitation data.
- Fatality rate was calculated as:
  
  Fatality Rate = Fatalities / Total Accidents

- A scatter plot was used to analyze the relationship between
  rainfall (mm) and fatality rate.

OBSERVATION:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- The scatter plot shows a wide distribution of data points.
- No clear increasing or decreasing trend is observed.
- Months with high rainfall do not consistently show higher fatality rates.

RESULT:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The analysis does NOT support the hypothesis.

There is no strong correlation between rainfall levels and
traffic accident fatality rates.


INTERPRETATION:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Rainfall alone does not significantly affect accident severity.

Possible explanations:
- Drivers may adapt their behavior in rainy conditions
- Road safety measures may reduce risk during bad weather
- Other factors (e.g., driver behavior, traffic density) may have
  a greater impact on fatality rates

================================================================================
CONCLUSION:
================================================================================

The results indicate that rainfall is not a strong predictor of
traffic accident severity in Turkey.

Although weather conditions may influence driving conditions,
they do not appear to significantly increase fatality rates on their own.

Further analysis with additional variables is recommended
to better understand the factors affecting accident severity.
